# Practitioner outreach plan

Purpose: invite medical, allied health, wellness, and similar practitioners to list their practice on Helfi.

Offer to use: 6 months free, then USD $4.95/month per listing. No card needed to start. The free period starts after the listing is approved.

Important rule: build the list first. Do not send emails while building the list. Send only after the list has been reviewed and approved.

## Safety rules

1. Do not buy email lists.
2. Do not use address-harvesting tools.
3. Do not scrape large batches of emails from websites.
4. Prefer opt-in and low-risk channels first.
5. If direct email is used, send only to clearly relevant practice or clinic contact addresses.
6. Keep proof of where each email came from.
7. Do not email anyone who has a "do not contact" or "no marketing" notice near their email address.
8. Include an easy opt-out line in every email.
9. Keep a do-not-contact list and never email opted-out people again.
10. Send slowly: 1 email per minute maximum.

## Best outreach channels

Use these first because they are safer than cold bulk email:

- Practitioner associations and professional groups
- Clinic contact forms
- LinkedIn messages to clinic owners or practice managers
- Newsletter sponsorships in practitioner communities
- Targeted ads for practitioners
- Existing Helfi network contacts
- Website sign-up page: https://www.helfi.ai/list-your-practice

## List fields

Use these columns:

- Country
- State or region
- City
- Practice name
- Practitioner name, if public and relevant
- Practitioner type
- Website
- Public email address
- Email type: clinic, admin, practitioner, personal, form only
- Source page URL
- Date found
- Do-not-contact notice found: yes or no
- Why the invite is relevant
- Safety basis
- Outreach status: not reviewed, approved, sent, replied, bounced, unsubscribed, do not contact
- Date sent
- Reply notes

## Country notes

Australia:
Use consent or very careful one-to-one outreach only. Avoid cold bulk lists. Every message must identify Helfi and include an unsubscribe option.

United States:
Commercial email can be sent if strict rules are followed. Use honest sender details, honest subject lines, a real postal address, and an easy opt-out.

United Kingdom:
Be careful with individual practitioners, sole traders, and personal email addresses. Company clinic addresses are lower risk, but still use an opt-out and keep a do-not-contact list.

Canada:
Be very careful. Publicly listed business emails may sometimes be used only when there is no "do not contact" notice and the message is clearly relevant to the person's business role. Keep proof.

New Zealand:
Use consent or safer channels. Do not use harvested addresses. Every message needs clear sender details and an unsubscribe option.

Other English-speaking countries:
Use the safest rule by default: only contact public business addresses where the message is directly relevant, include opt-out, and keep proof.

## Sending rules

- Send only after the list has been reviewed.
- Send 1 email per minute maximum.
- Send from a real Helfi email address.
- Use the same plain template, lightly personalised.
- Do not use misleading subject lines.
- Stop immediately for any person or practice that opts out.
- Do not follow up unless the person replies or clearly asks for more information.
